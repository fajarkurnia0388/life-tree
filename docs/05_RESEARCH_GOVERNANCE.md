# 05 — Research and Governance
## Evidence, Safety, Compliance, and Product Boundaries

> **Primary source of truth for research basis, legal/compliance posture, business rationale, accessibility rules, and ethical boundaries.**

---

## 1. Research Basis

Daoji / LifeTree is not built as pure intuition.
It stands on a practical combination of:
- behavior design,
- habit formation research,
- cognitive reflection practices,
- privacy-first product design,
- and psychologically safer motivation systems.

---

## 2. Behavioral Science Foundations

## 2.1 Habit formation
Relevant baseline:
- habit automaticity takes time;
- missed days are not identical to total failure;
- consistency matters more than dramatic bursts.

### Product implications
- no streak punishment
- cumulative framing is healthier
- tiny actions are valid
- relapse/re-entry must be normalized

## 2.2 Fogg Behavior Model / similar behavior design logic
Behavior depends on:
- motivation,
- ability,
- prompts.

### Product implications
- friction matters
- users often need a smaller step, not more shame
- Action of the Day should be realistic, not idealistic

## 2.3 Cognitive load
Users under overload cannot process overly complex systems well.

### Product implications
- dashboard must stay simple
- onboarding must remain progressive
- cultivation lore cannot overload the experience

## 2.4 Reflection as self-regulation
Journaling, structured reflection, and decision review can help users:
- notice patterns,
- become less reactive,
- make more deliberate decisions.

### Product implications
- journaling is not decorative
- thinking canvas must remain useful, not mystical fluff
- value mirror must lead to insight, not guilt

---

## 3. Why the Cultivation Theme Is Justified

The cultivation theme is not used because it is trendy.
It is justified because it mirrors the product’s structure:
- steady practice,
- setbacks as data,
- alignment with values,
- integration after difficulty,
- long-term transformation over quick hacks.

### Important constraint
Cultivation must remain a **meaning framework**, not a manipulative game system.

---

## 4. Product Safety Boundaries

## 4.1 Daoji is not therapy
The app may support reflection and self-regulation, but it must not present itself as:
- diagnosis,
- crisis treatment,
- psychotherapy substitute,
- or medical intervention.

## 4.2 Safety features must stay explicit
Critical safety flows must remain understandable without any lore knowledge.

Examples:
- `Safety Card` stays explicit
- crisis support text stays plain or dual-labeled
- low mood prompts stay clear

## 4.3 No overclaiming insights
If the app says “pattern” or “insight”, it must be proportionate to actual data.
No fake intelligence theater.

---

## 5. Privacy and Data Governance

## 5.1 Privacy posture
Default posture:
- local-first
- minimal dependency on remote services
- user data belongs to user

## 5.2 Data classes involved
Sensitive categories include:
- mood / journal data
- values and dilemmas
- decisions and reflections
- wellbeing-related prompts

## 5.3 Governance principles
- collect only what is useful
- avoid unnecessary remote sync complexity too early
- make export and reset transparent
- never monetize by selling behavioral data

## 5.4 Future cloud/sync caution
If sync is introduced later:
- privacy architecture must be explicit
- zero-knowledge or similar principles should be considered
- the local-first promise should not be quietly undermined

---

## 6. Compliance / Legal Posture

## 6.1 Age-sensitive design
The product already includes age band logic and disclaimer behavior.
This should remain a first-class concern.

## 6.2 Wellness disclaimers
Users must knowingly acknowledge that:
- the app supports reflection and daily orientation,
- but is not emergency or clinical treatment.

## 6.3 Child/teen caution
Any future youth-facing support mode must prioritize:
- privacy,
- non-exploitation,
- clarity of guardian/consent rules where applicable,
- no addictive gamification loops.

---

## 7. Accessibility Governance

## 7.1 Baseline
The product must remain accessible enough for users who:
- rely on semantics/screen readers,
- have visual sensitivity,
- are under stress,
- or struggle with high cognitive load.

## 7.2 Hard requirements
- semantic labels for important controls
- no color-only signaling
- no flashing effects
- readable contrast
- straightforward phrasing in critical flows

## 7.3 Cultivation-specific rule
Fantasy flavor must never reduce clarity for accessibility users.
If a poetic label is unclear, pair it with a plain subtitle.

---

## 8. Business Strategy Summary

## 8.1 Product trust before monetization pressure
A product like this survives on trust.
Therefore:
- manipulative retention loops are dangerous,
- ad-targeting would undermine the product’s moral core,
- premium should expand depth, not remove dignity from free users.

## 8.2 Reasonable monetization direction
A value-based freemium model is acceptable if:
- core orientation features remain usable for free,
- premium deepens optional insight/tools,
- privacy is not paywalled in abusive ways.

## 8.3 Marketplace caution
If templates/marketplace evolve:
- quality control matters
- avoid creator status hierarchies that reintroduce competition pressure
- keep community contribution healthy and bounded

---

## 9. Ethical Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Shame-driven motivation**
2. **False scarcity / false urgency**
3. **Productivity supremacism**
4. **Mental health cosplay** (pretending to provide more than the app truly can)
5. **Lore over clarity**
6. **Data extraction hidden behind wellness branding**

---

## 10. Governance Rules for Cultivation Layer

### Rule 1
Anti-Guilt outranks theme.

### Rule 2
Clarity outranks lore.

### Rule 3
Safety outranks aesthetics.

### Rule 4
Recovery outranks optimization.

### Rule 5
Long-term trust outranks short-term engagement spikes.

---

## 11. Research Questions Worth Preserving

As the product evolves, the team should keep asking:
- does cultivation framing increase user meaning, or only novelty?
- does hybrid language improve retention without confusion?
- does the app help users re-engage after setbacks more gently?
- do users feel more seen, or more judged, after advanced insights?
- does the marketplace strengthen growth, or create subtle comparison pressure?

These questions matter more than raw vanity metrics.

---

## 12. Measurement Philosophy

Success should not be reduced to:
- daily opens,
- session length,
- or number of habits created.

More meaningful indicators include:
- re-engagement after missed days,
- sustained use without shame spikes,
- healthy use of recovery mode,
- value alignment adoption,
- user trust in journaling and reflection features.

## 14. Final Principle

Daoji should feel wiser than a habit tracker, but more grounded than a fantasy system.
Its research and governance model must make sure that:
- growth stays humane,
- reflection stays honest,
- privacy stays real,
- and the cultivation theme serves the person — not the other way around.
